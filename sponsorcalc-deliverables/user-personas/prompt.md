# SponsorCalc - User Personas

## Primary Persona: Mid-Tier Creator "Maya"

### Demographics
- **Age**: 26
- **Location**: Austin, TX
- **Education**: Marketing degree, some business coursework
- **Income**: $45K annually ($35K from sponsorships, $10K other sources)

### Platform Presence
- **Instagram**: 85K followers (lifestyle/fashion niche)
- **TikTok**: 120K followers
- **YouTube**: 15K subscribers
- **Engagement rate**: 3.2% average across platforms

### Creator Business Profile
- **Full-time creator** for 18 months
- **Brand deals**: 3-4 per month
- **Average deal size**: $800-1,200
- **Deal types**: Instagram posts, story placements, TikTok videos

### Pain Points
1. **Pricing uncertainty**: "I never know if I'm charging enough or too much"
2. **Brand negotiations**: "When brands push back on rates, I usually just accept their counter-offer"
3. **Professional credibility**: "I need to look more professional when approaching bigger brands"
4. **Time management**: "I spend too much time researching what others charge"

### Goals & Motivations
- **Increase revenue**: Want to reach $60K annually within 12 months
- **Professional growth**: Transition from lifestyle to business-focused content
- **Efficiency**: Streamline sponsorship process to focus on content creation
- **Credibility**: Be taken seriously by major brands

### Technology Usage
- **Primary device**: iPhone (90% of social media activity)
- **Tools used**: Later (scheduling), Canva (design), basic spreadsheet for tracking
- **Tech comfort**: High for social platforms, moderate for business tools
- **Decision factors**: Ease of use, mobile-first, visible ROI

### Information Sources
- **Creator communities**: Facebook groups, Discord servers
- **Education**: YouTube tutorials, creator economy newsletters
- **Inspiration**: Other creators in similar niches
- **Industry news**: Social media blogs, platform updates

### How She Discovers Solutions
1. **Problem recognition**: Realizes she's potentially undercharging
2. **Research phase**: Googles "Instagram sponsorship rates" and asks creator groups
3. **Solution evaluation**: Tries free calculators, finds them inadequate
4. **Purchase decision**: Needs to see clear ROI and professional output

### SponsorCalc Usage Journey
1. **Discovery**: Finds through creator community recommendation
2. **Trial**: Uses free tier to calculate basic rates
3. **Value realization**: Sees her rates are 30% below market
4. **Conversion trigger**: Needs professional rate card for major brand outreach
5. **Ongoing use**: Monthly rate updates, deal tracking, negotiation scripts

## Secondary Persona: Agency Manager "David"

### Demographics
- **Age**: 32
- **Location**: Los Angeles, CA
- **Education**: MBA, Marketing focus
- **Role**: Talent Manager at mid-size influencer agency

### Professional Profile
- **Company size**: 50-person agency
- **Creator roster**: 25 active creators (10K-2M followers)
- **Revenue responsibility**: $2M annually
- **Experience**: 5 years in influencer marketing

### Daily Responsibilities
- **Rate negotiations** with brands for multiple creators
- **Performance analysis** and creator development
- **Client relationship management**
- **Market research** for pricing strategies

### Pain Points
1. **Scale challenges**: "I need to benchmark rates for 25 different creators efficiently"
2. **Consistency**: "Each creator negotiation feels like starting from scratch"
3. **Client expectations**: "Brands expect data-backed rate justifications"
4. **Time constraints**: "I can't research each deal individually"

### Goals & Motivations
- **Revenue optimization**: Maximize revenue per creator
- **Process efficiency**: Standardize rate-setting across roster
- **Client satisfaction**: Keep both creators and brands happy
- **Professional credibility**: Maintain agency reputation in market

### Technology Usage
- **Primary tools**: CRM, Excel, email, industry platforms
- **Decision factors**: Scalability, integration capabilities, team features
- **Budget authority**: Can approve $100-500/month tools
- **Implementation needs**: Team access, reporting features

### How He Evaluates Solutions
1. **ROI calculation**: Clear revenue impact measurement
2. **Efficiency gains**: Time saved per creator/deal
3. **Professional output**: Client-ready materials
4. **Scalability**: Works for entire roster

### SponsorCalc Agency Use Case
1. **Bulk rate analysis** for multiple creators
2. **Standardized rate cards** for all clients
3. **Benchmarking** against industry standards
4. **White-label materials** for client presentations

## Anti-Persona: Enterprise Brand Manager

### Why Not a Target
- **Different needs**: Focused on brand-side requirements
- **Existing solutions**: Already has agency relationships and tools
- **Budget process**: Complex procurement, longer sales cycles
- **Value perception**: Sees creator tools as competitive threat

### Key Differences
- Evaluates from brand perspective, not creator perspective
- Needs integration with existing marketing stack
- Requires enterprise-level security and compliance
- Different success metrics (campaign performance vs. creator income)
